### Description for Site

The Anand Karaj — "blissful union" — is the Sikh wedding ceremony held in the presence of the Guru Granth Sahib. The ceremony includes Kirtan (devotional music) and four lavan (rounds) signifying the couple's spiritual and worldly journey together. Modest dress is required; head coverings must be worn inside the Gurudwara (provided at the door). Following the ceremony, all guests are invited to Langar, a vegetarian community meal served in the tent.

### Attire Notes

- Modest dress required
- Head must be covered inside the Gurudwara (scarves/patkas provided)
- Remove shoes before entering (socks recommended)
- No leather inside the Gurudwara
